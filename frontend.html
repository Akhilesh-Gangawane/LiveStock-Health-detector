Disease Knowledge Base

Bovine Respiratory Disease

Common respiratory infection affecting cattle. Symptoms include coughing, nasal discharge, and fever. Early treatment with antibiotics is crucial for recovery and preventing spread to other animals.

Prevention: Proper ventilation, vaccination, quarantine of new animals

Foot and Mouth Disease

Highly contagious viral disease affecting cloven-hoofed animals. Causes fever, blisters in mouth and on feet. Requires immediate isolation and veterinary intervention.

Prevention: Vaccination, biosecurity measures, movement restrictions

Mastitis

Inflammation of mammary gland, common in dairy cows. Causes swelling, heat, and changes in milk quality. Significantly affects milk production and animal welfare.

Prevention: Proper milking hygiene, teat dipping, dry cow therapy

Anthrax

Serious bacterial infection affecting all warm-blooded animals. Can cause sudden death without warning signs. Vaccination is the most effective prevention method.

Prevention: Annual vaccination, proper carcass disposal, quarantine

Brucellosis

Bacterial infection causing reproductive problems in cattle. Can cause abortions and infertility. Also zoonotic, meaning it can transfer to humans through contact.

Prevention: Vaccination, testing, culling infected animals

Milk Fever (Hypocalcemia)

Calcium deficiency in dairy cows around calving time. Causes weakness, inability to stand, and can be life-threatening. Requires immediate calcium supplementation.

Prevention: Proper nutrition, calcium supplementation, monitoring
